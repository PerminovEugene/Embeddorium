Social Tax and Pension Contributions

Social tax is paid by employers on wages and by self-employed persons on their business income. The rate is 33%, of which 20% goes to pension insurance and 13% to health insurance.

Employees who have joined the mandatory funded pension scheme (II pillar) contribute an additional 2% from their gross salary. The state adds a further 4% on top. See the II pillar joining guide for enrollment instructions.

Workers who opted out of the II pillar between 2020 and 2021 may re-enroll. Details are covered in the re-enrollment FAQ. The deadline for the next enrollment window is described there as well.

Unemployment insurance contributions are collected by the Estonian Unemployment Insurance Fund. Employees pay 1.6% and employers pay 0.8% of gross wages. The full contribution table is at contribution rates.

Self-employed persons can register via the e-MTA portal. Instructions for registering as a sole proprietor are on the sole proprietor registration page. VAT registration thresholds are explained on the VAT registration page.

Annual reporting deadlines and the tax calendar are available at the EMTA tax calendar. Late payments are subject to interest charges described in the interest rate guide.
